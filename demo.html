Research Gaps We Identified & Filled

❌ Gap 1: Domain Knowledge

The baseline DTFD-MIL uses ResNet-50 pretrained on ImageNet. ResNet knows dogs and cats — not carcinomas. When you give it only 40 training bags of medical tissue, it cannot generalize.


Our Fix: We replaced ResNet-50 with PLIP — a CLIP-style model trained on 200K PubMed pathology images. Even with 40 bags, PLIP's embeddings already separate malignant from benign tissue in latent space.

❌ Gap 2: Random Pseudo-Bag Splitting

The baseline uses torch.randperm to randomly split WSI patches into pseudo-bags. This fractures tumor clusters across multiple bags, diluting the diagnostic signal and confusing the attention network.


Our Fix: We use SAM + K-Means Clustering to group structurally similar patches together. Tumor cells naturally cluster into the same semantic pseudo-bag, creating a pure, detectable signal.

⚙️ Technical Details

  • Framework: PyTorch + HuggingFace Transformers
  • Optimizer: Adam (lr=1e-4, wd=1e-4)
  • Epochs: 50 | Pseudo-bags: 5 | Distill: AFS
  • Dataset: PatchCamelyon (1,000 images / 50 bags)
  • Hardware: AMD Ryzen, 16GB RAM (CPU only)

⚠️ Hardware Challenge Overcome

SAM's ViT encoder scales images to 1024×1024 internally, causing a catastrophic RAM spike. Batching just 10 patches consumed 8GB of RAM — crashing our 16GB system.


Solution: We re-engineered the pipeline to process images one at a time, converting a 10-minute operation into a sequential process that completes within hardware limits.
